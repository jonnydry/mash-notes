# Code of conduct

Mash aims to be a welcoming, practical open-source project.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, specific, and patient.
- Critique ideas and implementations without attacking people.
- Assume good intent while remaining clear about impact.
- Make space for contributors with different experience levels and backgrounds.
- Protect private information and never request real sensitive documents for a reproduction.
- Accept maintainers' decisions about project scope and product direction.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, or sexualized conduct.
- Personal attacks, trolling, or sustained disruption.
- Publishing another person's private information without permission.
- Using project spaces for spam, promotion, or unrelated campaigning.
- Retaliating against someone who reports a concern.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove contributions and may temporarily or permanently restrict participation when behavior harms the project or its community.

For conduct concerns that should not be public, use GitHub's private vulnerability-reporting channel and state clearly that the report concerns community conduct rather than a software vulnerability. Reports will be handled as privately as the platform permits.
